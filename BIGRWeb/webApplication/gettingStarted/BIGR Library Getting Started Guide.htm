12     Glossary 

% Cellular Stroma (TCS)               

Percent distribution of tumor hypercellular stroma cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

% Hypo/Acellular Stroma (TAS)   

Percent distribution of tumor hypo-/acellular stroma cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

% Lesion (LSN)   Percent distribution of non-neoplastic lesional cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

% Necrosis (NEC)      

Percent distribution of necrotic cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

% Normal (NRM)       

Percent distribution of normal cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

% Tumor (TMR)         

Percent distribution of tumor cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Additional Pathology Findings Notes                

Notes as added by the pathologist at the donor institution for the additional pathology findings of the pathology report. These notes provide additional descriptions of important information that does not easily fit any of the currently structured fields.

 

Age at Surgery     Age of the donor at the time of the surgery for this case.  Ages are exact or modified according to IRB protocols at the Donor Institutions, and in some instances may be the actual age plus or minus 5 years. For more details or if exact age is relevant to your research, contact Ardais Customer Service.

 

AJCC                    “The American Joint Committee on Cancer was formally established in 1959. Its goal is to formulate and publish systems of classification of cancer, including staging and end results reporting, which will be acceptable to and used by the medical profession for selecting the most effective treatment, determining prognosis, and continuing evaluation of cancer control measures.”

                              From the website: http://www.cancerstaging.org/

 

AJCC Cancer Staging Manual, Sixth Edition, ordering information

See the URL: http://cancerstaging.org/initiatives.html#3

 

 

Appearance          The appearance of a sample is based on either gross examination (before Ardais Pathology Verification) or microscopic evaluation (after Ardais Pathology Verification).

 

Ardais Pathology Verification Report              

All samples supplied by Ardais are verified by an Ardais pathologist.  This process is called Ardais Pathology Verification.

 

Ardais Pathology Verification RNA Report

All samples supplied by Ardais are verified by an Ardais pathologist. This process is called Ardais Pathology Verification. All RNA is generated from samples that are pathology verified.

 

ASM                     Surgical specimens are dissected into Ardais Sample Modules (ASMs) that vary in size, depending on the volume of bankable tissue. Each module is further sub-divided into individual samples by following strict guidelines that preserve the spatial relationship of the samples within the module. Modules may contain either frozen OCT-embedded format (FR) or formalin fixed paraffin embedded tissue (PA).

 

Bioanalyzer Ratio (18S/28S)

Calculated ratio between the 18S and 28S ribosomal RNA peaks using the Agilent 2100 Bioanalyzer. The area under the curve of each peak is used to calculate the ratio.

 

Case Diagnosis    The identification of a disease based on the histopathological evaluation of its signs and symptoms, as defined by the pathologist at the donor institution. Expand your search, if desired, by choosing all categories and subcategories that apply.

 

Case Diagnosis contains                

The samples returned will have case diagnoses that contain the words, partial words, or phrases specified. You may use the "*" character as a wildcard to enhance your search. Use combinations of wildcards to lead, follow or enclose text e.g.; "*carcinoma", "carcinoma*" and "*carcinoma*".

 

Case ID                A unique alphanumeric code assigned each time a donor comes into a medical institution for a new surgery. Case IDs start with CI for cases where informed consent was obtained (known as linked cases) and CU for unlinked cases. When this term is hotlinked (underlined) it takes you to the Donor Profile and the Donor Case Report.

 

Case Profile Note                           

A free text field that will be populated with information abstracted about the case. These notes provide additional descriptions of important information that does not easily fit any of the currently structured fields. Additional information requested by users about a Case will appear here.

 

Composition         Composition values reflect the percent cell distribution found during Ardais Pathology Verification, a process of sample validation that relies on microscopic examination of each sample. Enable your search by using this parameter selectively. You should not use Microscopic or Gross Appearance values in conjunction with Composition values. You should conduct separate searches for Tumor, Lesional, or Normal samples. You may limit the amount of Necrosis with any of these searches. Additionally, you may limit the amount of stroma while searching for tumor samples.

 

Distant Metastasis      

The absence or presence of distant metastasis. For the majority of the samples in BIGR™ Library, Ardais does not have knowledge about this attribute, since this information is gathered post surgery and often at the outpatient clinic. In these cases, Distant Metastasis will be Mx, meaning not assessed. When the knowledge related to this attribute is limited in this way, Ardais can only determine the minimum stage grouping. Expand your search by choosing all that apply.

 

Donor Case Report     

The pathology report from the Donor Institution often contains several sections that refer to several different tissues resected during surgery. Some of these tissues are available in the repository, some are not.

 

Donor ID              A unique alphanumeric code assigned to the donor using a de-identification process to preserve donor privacy. It is assigned once and does not change if the donor has subsequent visits to a medical institution.

 

Donor Profile Note      

A free text field that will be populated with information abstracted from Clinical Data and may include relevant socioeconomic information such as smoking and familial histories. These notes provide additional descriptions of important information that does not easily fit any of the currently structured fields. Additional information requested by users about a Donor will appear here.

 

Donor/Case Pathology Notes Keyword Search                  

This option lets you search the Donor/Case Pathology Notes recorded by the pathologist at the Donor Institution.  Samples returned by this query will contain the word specified. Enter whole words, separated by spaces, or use an asterisk (*) as a wildcard character. As one example, *moking* returns the Smoking (smoking) History if it is recorded. Complete data may not yet be entered. Please request additional data from Ardais Customer Service.

 

Data can be found under Case ID; Donor Profile; Donor Profile Note or Case ID; Donor Case Report; Case Pathology Notes or Pathology Section Notes.

 

Extended Composition/Comments

Composition and additional structure information for the sample as determined during Ardais Pathology Verification, shown on the Ardais Pathology Verification page. Also shown as Pathology Verification Notes on the Sample Selection Query and Sample Selection Results pages.

 

Format                  Tissue samples are available in either frozen OCT-embedded format (FR) or formalin-fixed paraffin embedded format (PA). Frozen samples are stored at -86oC. Formalin-fixed samples are processed and embedded in paraffin.

 

Note: This parameter applies only to Tissue samples. Searches for RNA samples are NOT affected by the value of this parameter.

 

Gel Electrophoresis     

Representative image of total RNA obtained from a particular tissue run on a 1% denaturing agarose gel or run using the Agilent 2100 Bioanalyzer. Ardais RNA quality can be determined through visual inspection of the electropherogram. For total RNA purified by Ardais, the electropherogram depicts two prominent peaks representing the 18S and 28S ribosomal RNA.

 

 

Gender                  Gender of the donor. Choices are: Any, Male, or Female. Any includes Male, Female, Other, and Unknown. Other indicates the patient is, for example, a hermaphrodite.  Unknown indicates those rare circumstances when gender is not recorded in the medical record.

 

Gross Appearance       

Appearance values at the gross level.  These are assigned to samples at the grossing station immediately after removal, and are either Gross Diseased, Gross Normal, Gross Mixed, or Unknown.  Expand your search, if desired, by including Unknowns, particularly for Adenocarcinoma of the Prostate.

 

Note: This parameter applies only to Tissue samples. Searches for RNA samples are NOT affected by the value of this parameter.

 

Histologic/Nuclear Grade

The results of pathologic grading. See AJCC manual. Expand your search by choosing all that apply.

 

Images                  A link to images used by the Ardais pathologist to validate the diagnosis, tissue, and appearance of the sample. This link also provides access to a summary of other data about this sample.

 

Inflammation        This is a component of lesion. This field includes information only about inflammatory cells.  Other lesional components could be "% Fibrocystic Change to Breast" or % Hyperplasia. 

 

Linked Cases       Cases (and their associated samples) that map permanently to Donor ID.  Donor consent must be obtained before a case is linked and all of the patient data entered. Expand your search, if necessary and scientifically appropriate, by using unlinked cases as well as linked.

 

LSN                      Percent distribution of lesional cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Lymph Node Stage      

The extent of regional lymph node metastasis according to pathologic assessment. Expand your search by choosing all that apply.

 

Microscopic Appearance               

Appearance values at the microscopic level.  These are assigned to samples during Ardais Pathology Verification, and are either Tumor, Lesional, or Normal. Expand your search, if desired, by using Gross Appearance values as well.

 

Minimum Stage Grouping

The stage grouping used is based on pathologic assessment of the TNM values (pTNM, see AJCC manual). Each stage group is relatively homogeneous with respect to survival and the survival rates of these groups for each cancer site are distinct. Minimum Stage grouping is used as a predictor of survival and as a guide to treatment. Expand your search by choosing all that apply. {Ami did you invert the middle 2 sentences before? And should we do that now?}

 

NEC                      Percent distribution of necrotic cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Non Tumor Structures with %       

This section contains one or more non-tumor components based on the type of tissue involved as defined during Ardais Pathology Verification by Ardais. For example: Lung would include "% Alveoli", "% Bronchioles" and "% Fibrovascular Septa". Other tissues would support other structures.

 

NRM                    Percent distribution of normal cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Path Verification Notes                  

Pathology Verification Notes are recorded by an Ardais pathologist upon pathology verification of the sample.

 

Pathologic TNM  The concatenated values for the pathologic assessment of the TNM values. These values are the basis for the minimum stage grouping.

 

Pathology Assessment

The staging and grading information provided by the Donor Institution.  Ardais uses the standards of the American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) as defined in the AJCC Cancer Staging Manual; Fifth and Sixth editions.

 

Pathology Image (20X)                   

An image (at 20X magnification) which is representative of the sample as determined by Ardais Pathology Verification.

 

Pathology Image (4X) 

An image (at 4X magnification) which is representative of the sample as determined by Ardais Pathology Verification.

 

Pathology Section Notes                

Notes as added by the pathologist at the donor institution for the primary section of the pathology report. These notes provide additional descriptions of important information that does not easily fit any of the currently structured fields.

 

Pathology Verification Notes

Pathology Verification Notes are recorded by an Ardais pathologist based on pathology verification of the sample, shown in Sample Selection Results. Also shown as “Extended Composition/Comments” on the Ardais Pathology Verification Report page.

 

Pathology Verification Notes keyword Search

This option lets you search the Ardais Pathology Verification Notes that were recorded by the Ardais pathologist who verified the accuracy of the diagnosis, tissue, and appearance for the sample.  Samples returned by this query will contain the word specified. Enter whole words, separated by spaces, or use an asterisk (*) as a wildcard character. As one example, use the term Bronchioles to find samples containing this structural component.

 

Note: This parameter applies only to Tissue samples. Searches for RNA samples are NOT affected by the value of this parameter.

 

Pathology Verification Status        

Indicates whether or not an Ardais pathologist has verified the accuracy of the diagnosis, tissue, and appearance for the sample.

 

All samples supplied by Ardais are verified by an Ardais pathologist.  However, this verification might not have been completed yet for every sample in BIGR™ Library. Ardais recommends first searching for samples that have already been verified, because they can be shipped more quickly. Expand your search, if desired, by selecting "Show All Samples". All samples will be verified by an Ardais pathologist before they are shipped.

 

Note: This parameter applies only to Tissue samples. Searches for RNA samples are NOT affected by the value of this parameter.

 

Prep Number        The preparation number for this derivative product from its parent case.

 

Primary Diagnosis from Donor Institution Pathology Report               

The identification of a disease based on the histopathological evaluation of its signs and symptoms, as defined by the pathologist at the donor institution according to the primary section of the pathology report.

 

Primary Section    The primary section is the most severe or advanced diagnosis that the donor presents and is the reason that the surgery was undertaken.

 

Primary Site of Finding                   

The tissue or body region in which the disease is found according to the primary section of the Donor Institution's pathology report.

 

Primary Tissue of Origin of Diagnosis             

The tissue in which the disease arises according to the primary section of the Donor Institution's pathology report.

 

Remove                Use this checkbox to indicate which samples you want to remove from your hold list. 

 

Then use the “Remove Selected Items” button at the bottom of this page to remove from your Hold List the samples that are selected.  

 

The microscope icon indicates that Ardais Pathology Results are available.  Click the microscope icon to view the results.

 

RT-PCR               Representative image of an RT-PCR reaction obtained from Dnased total RNA from a particular tissue in presence and absence of reverse transcriptase. The negative reaction controls for the presence of genomic DNA where as the positive reaction demonstrates that the purified RNA can be manipulated enzymatically. Human b-actin primers are used for the experiment and 35 cycles of amplification are performed for all samples.

 

Sample ID            A unique alphanumeric code assigned to each sample extracted or derived from a Case. The first two characters identify the sample type (e.g. 'FR' for OCT-embedded frozen samples, 'PA' for formalin-fixed paraffin samples, 'RN' for RNA, etc.).

 

Sample Pathology contains            

The samples returned will have case diagnoses that contain the words, partial words, or phrases specified. You may use the "*" character as a wildcard to enhance your search. Use combinations of wildcards to lead, follow or enclose text e.g.; "*carcinoma", "carcinoma*" and "*carcinoma*".

 

Sample Pathology from Ardais Pathology Verification       

The pathologic description of an individual sample based on the microscopic evaluation of the sample, as defined by an Ardais pathologist for the sample being verified. Expand your search, if desired, by choosing all categories and subcategories that apply.

 

Section Diagnosis from Donor Institution Pathology Report                

The diagnosis as recorded in the primary section of the pathology report submitted by the donor institution.

 

Select                    Use this checkbox to indicate which samples you want to select. 

 

Then use the “Place Items on Hold” button at the bottom of this page to put the samples that you selected on Hold. The number of samples currently selected and the number of samples on Hold is shown at the top of this page.  

 

The microscope icon indicates that Ardais Pathology Verification results are available.  Click the microscope icon to view the results.

 

Site of Finding      The tissue or body region in which the disease is found.

 

Site of Finding contains/or does NOT contain  

The samples returned will have tissues that contain or do NOT contain the words, partial words, or phrases specified. You may use the "*" character as a wildcard to enhance your search. Use combinations of wildcards to lead, follow or enclose text e.g.; "*breast", "breast*" and "*breast*".

 

TAS                       Percent distribution of tumor hypo-/acellular stroma cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

TCS                       Percent distribution of tumor hypercellular stroma cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Tissue of Origin of Diagnosis        

The tissue in which the disease arises.

 

Tissue of Origin of Diagnosis contains/or does NOT contain                

The samples returned will have tissues that contain or do NOT contain the words, partial words, or phrases specified. You may use the "*" character as a wildcard to enhance your search. Use combinations of wildcards to lead, follow or enclose text e.g.; "*breast", "breast*" and "*breast*".

 

TMR                     Percent distribution of tumor cells in the sample based on microscopic examination.

 

Tumor Stage         The extent of the primary tumor according to pathologic assessment. Expand your search by choosing all that apply.
